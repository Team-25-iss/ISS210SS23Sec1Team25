How Racially Diverse Schools and Classrooms Can Benefit All Students

The article discusses the numerous advantages of promoting racial diversity in schools and classrooms, highlighting its positive impact on academic achievement, social skills, and civic engagement for all students. However, the authors also address the significant disparities in extracurricular participation among students of color and low-income families, which can negatively affect their academic performance and social development. Research has shown that participation in extracurricular activities can enhance students' self-esteem, leadership skills, and academic success. To address this issue, schools should offer more affordable and accessible options for extracurricular activities, provide transportation for students who face challenges in attending after-school activities, and actively encourage diverse participation. Additionally, schools should address systemic inequities such as funding disparities and cultural biases to promote greater equity in extracurricular opportunities. By promoting diversity and inclusion in all aspects of school life, educators can help foster a supportive and enriching environment that benefits all students.

The Engagement Gap

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


Regarding socioeconomic class’s relation to education, most discussion revolves around differences in test scores. However, this paper suggests that investigating the differences in extracurricular involvement between social classes is just as important. The authors evaluate statistics from previous studies on this subject and reveal that the disparity between working-class and upper-middle class students’ participation in extracurriculars is growing at an alarming rate.This divide is mostly due to the growing class stratification in general. On a community level, schools in wealthier areas can invest more money into extracurricular programs and can also benefit from philanthropy by community members. This is in contrast to poorer schools that may have to cut back on spending for clubs and sports programs, or be unable to offer them entirely. On an individual level, another issue is the emergence of “pay-to-play” sports programs - ones that require fees to participate in. According to the authors, “one in three sports-playing kids from homes with annual income of $60,000 or less dropped out because of the increased cost, as compared to one in ten kids from families with incomes over $60,000.” This calls for policymakers, parents, and schools to take immediate and urgent action to reduce this disparity in order to reduce educational inequity for our children.

Racism in High School Sports

In this article by the NAACP, the organization makes a pledge to identify racial harassment and outcasting in High School sports and to put a stop to it wherever they see it. It highlights how one black student at Buchanan High school received “monkey/ape noises from a student of the opposing school, and how a Latina student from the same high school was barked at. Both occurring during a penalty kick the victims of the abuse were taking. The article also describes how high school faculty need to take a bigger step in protecting their students from racially motivated abuse.
